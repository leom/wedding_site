London

image source

So, London, right? I'll be honest, if you've never been, it's all kinds of rad. There are a million things to do, and there's no lack of information out there around the city. Here are some apps and tips if you're travelling into London, just to make your time a little easier.

First things first, if you don't have an international data plan turn off roaming on your phone. There's no lack of free WiFi in London (in addition to a Starbucks on every other corner), so roaming is unnecessary. Turning off roaming on your iPhone is easy, and while I've only used an iPhone, the these Android directions look just as easy.

Except Google Maps, none of the following apps require an internet connection (but even Google Maps lets you preload your maps, so as long as you plan ahead you'll be all set).

Helpful iPhone Apps

Currency

TimeOut London

TimeOut London has a great list of sights to see and things to do all in their app. This alone should be enough, but to top it off it's got a GPS enabled map that doesn't need to use the internet to function. If (wait - correction - When) you get lost) this is invaluable.

Tube Map

The London Underground isn't nearly as confusing as everyone says but even still, having a tube map that will route you from station to station (even allowing for a detour) can be indispensible.

Google Maps

Google Maps is easy the best maps app out there. In addition to preloading your images, the directions and GPS positioning are second to none. Load up the parts of London you're interested in while you're on WiFi, and you'll be set.
